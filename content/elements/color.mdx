## Overview

Color is one of the most immediately perceived properties in visual design. Before a user reads a word or recognizes a shape, color has already communicated mood, hierarchy, and brand. It is also one of the most misused tools in a designer's kit — reaching for color to solve problems that structure and typography should handle first.

## Why It Matters

Color carries meaning both culturally and perceptually. Used well, it accelerates comprehension and creates emotional resonance. Used poorly, it creates confusion, excludes users with visual impairments, and undermines the clarity of a design.
