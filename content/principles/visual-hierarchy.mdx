Visual hierarchy is the principle that elements in a composition should be arranged to communicate their relative importance. It is the difference between a design that guides and one that merely presents.

Hierarchy is established through contrast — differences in size, weight, color, spacing, and position all signal to the viewer which elements demand attention first, second, and third.

## Why It Matters

Without hierarchy, every element competes equally for attention. The result is visual noise, not communication. A strong hierarchy lets users scan efficiently, find what they need, and understand the structure of a page before reading a single word.
